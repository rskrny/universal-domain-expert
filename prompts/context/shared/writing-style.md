# Writing Style — Mandatory Standards

> These rules apply to ALL written output across ALL domains. No exceptions.
> Every sentence that violates these rules gets rewritten before delivery.
> This file is referenced by AGENTS.md at Stage 4 (Create) and Stage 5 (Review).

---

## Hard Rejections

These patterns cause immediate sentence rejection. Rewrite from scratch.

### 1. Semicolons
Never use semicolons. If you're reaching for one, you have two sentences. Write two sentences.

### 2. Em Dashes
Never use em dashes (—). They're a crutch for weak sentence structure. If you need a pause, restructure the sentence. If you need to insert a clause, use commas or rewrite as its own sentence.

### 3. Contrast Through Negation
Never use "not" to set up a contrast. The pattern "not X, but Y" or "not just X, rather Y" is rejected every time.

**Rejected:** "This is not a framework, but a decision tool."
**Rewritten:** "This is a decision tool."

**Rejected:** "We don't need more data, we need better questions."
**Rewritten:** "We need better questions, and the data will follow."

Say what something IS. Skip what it isn't.

## AI Slop Detection

If a sentence sounds like it could appear in any LinkedIn post, ChatGPT default output, or corporate press release, it's slop. Delete it and write something a human would actually say.

### Banned Phrases (auto-reject)

**Filler openers:**
- "It's important to note that..."
- "It's worth mentioning that..."
- "In today's [adjective] world/landscape/environment..."
- "When it comes to..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "In order to..." (just say "to")
- "It goes without saying..." (then don't say it)
- "Needless to say..." (same)

**Empty transitions:**
- "Furthermore" / "Moreover" / "Additionally"
- "That being said" / "With that said"
- "On the other hand" (just make the second point)
- "Having said that"
- "It should be noted"

**Performative depth:**
- "This is a nuanced/complex topic" (be nuanced, don't announce it)
- "There are many factors to consider" (then consider them, don't narrate)
- "This requires careful consideration" (then consider it carefully)
- "The landscape is evolving" / "rapidly changing" / "ever-evolving"

**Pseudo-precision:**
- "Leverage" (when you mean "use")
- "Utilize" (when you mean "use")
- "Facilitate" (when you mean "help" or "enable")
- "Optimize" (when you mean "improve")
- "Synergy" / "synergize" / "synergistic" (say what actually happens)
- "Holistic" (say what you actually mean)
- "Robust" (describe the actual property)
- "Comprehensive" (describe what's actually covered)
- "Streamline" (say what changes)
- "Actionable insights" (just give the insights)
- "Best practices" (say the actual practice)
- "Paradigm shift" (describe the actual change)

**Hedging that adds nothing:**
- "It might be worth considering..."
- "One could argue that..."
- "Perhaps it would be beneficial to..."
- "There's a case to be made for..."

If you're uncertain, say so directly: "I'm uncertain about X because Y." Don't wrap uncertainty in soft language.

**Filler closers:**
- "In conclusion" (the reader knows it's the end)
- "To summarize" / "In summary" (just end)
- "Moving forward" (meaningless)
- "At the end of the day" (say something specific)
- "All things considered" (you just considered them)

## What Good Writing Looks Like

### Short sentences win.
The best business writing uses short, direct sentences. If a sentence has more than 25 words, consider splitting it. Not every sentence needs to be short. But most should be.

### Lead with the point.
The first sentence of any paragraph should be the point. Supporting detail follows. The reader should be able to read first sentences only and get the full picture.

### Use concrete language.
Replace abstract words with specific ones. "Significant revenue opportunity" tells the reader nothing. "$12M annual opportunity at 40% margins" tells them everything.

### Cut the word count in half, then cut again.
First drafts are always too long. The quality of writing is inversely proportional to its word count. If you can say it in 8 words, don't use 16.

### Active voice.
"The team completed the analysis" beats "The analysis was completed by the team." Active voice is shorter, clearer, and more direct.

### One idea per paragraph.
If a paragraph covers two ideas, it's two paragraphs. This makes scanning easy and forces clarity.

## Review Protocol

During Stage 5 (Quality Assurance), every deliverable goes through this writing check:

1. **Search for semicolons.** Replace with periods.
2. **Search for em dashes.** Restructure the sentence.
3. **Search for "not X, but Y" patterns.** Rewrite to state what something IS.
4. **Read every sentence aloud.** If it sounds like a press release, rewrite it.
5. **Check for banned phrases.** Delete and rewrite.
6. **Check word count.** Can any sentence lose 30% of its words without losing meaning? Do it.
7. **Check for passive voice.** Convert to active where possible.
8. **Check opening sentences.** Does each paragraph open with its main point?
